GC Method Development

Fatty acids are essential components of the human body as the energy source vital for healthy metabolism, acting as significant cell membranes and precursors of eicosanoid Hormones. An adequate intake of omega-3 fatty acids may prevent the onset of chronic diseases, such as cardiovascular diseases, relief from the symptoms associated with ulcerative colitis and joint pain. Gas chromatography with flame ionization detection (GC-FID) has been used to quantify fatty acids in fish. for determining omega-3 fatty acids (DHA and EPA).

Ampicillin sodium

Ampicillin is a semi-synthetic penicillin with a broad spectrum action, which was produced in 1961 from the addition of different side chains to the penicillin nucleus. It was the first semi-synthetic penicillin that showed activity against Gram-negative bacilli, opening the field of broad spectrum penicillin. Thus, Ampicillin preserves the penicillin antibacterial spectrum against Gram-positive microorganisms and it is even more active against Gram-negative, due to its increased ability to penetrate the outer membrane of these bacteria Ampicillin sodium (AMPI) has the same action and use of Ampicillin, considering that the sodium form is used for intramuscular and intravenous administrations. Several methods have been reported in the literature for the analysis of Ampicillin sodium such as: High performance liquid chromatographic (HPLC).

What is Clarity Software?

What is the Atomic Absorption spectrophotometer ?

What is the purpose of performing the analysis using the TOC instrument?

Carbon content is one of the most important parameters measured in various types of solutions, from commercial drinking water to industrial wastewater. Total organic carbon (TOC) analyzers are instruments used to analyze the organic carbon content in these water or liquid solution.
